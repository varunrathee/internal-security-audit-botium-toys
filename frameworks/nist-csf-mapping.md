# 🧩 NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) Mapping

This document maps the findings of the Botium Toys internal security audit
to the five core functions of the NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF).

---

## 1️⃣ Identify
**Gaps Identified:**
- No formal asset inventory or classification
- Excessive access to sensitive data
- Lack of risk-based asset management

**Impact:**
Inability to understand which assets are critical and at risk.

---

## 2️⃣ Protect
**Gaps Identified:**
- No encryption for customer and payment data
- Weak password policies
- No role-based access controls
- No password management system

**Impact:**
High risk of unauthorized access and data exposure.

---

## 3️⃣ Detect
**Gaps Identified:**
- No Intrusion Detection System (IDS)
- Limited monitoring of legacy systems

**Impact:**
Delayed detection of security incidents and threats.

---

## 4️⃣ Respond
**Strengths:**
- Incident notification plan exists for EU customers

**Gaps Identified:**
- No documented incident response procedures

**Impact:**
Inconsistent response to security incidents.

---

## 5️⃣ Recover
**Gaps Identified:**
- No disaster recovery plans
- No data backups

**Impact:**
High risk of prolonged downtime and data loss.

---

## 📌 Summary
Botium Toys shows significant gaps across all NIST CSF functions,
particularly in Identify, Protect, Detect, and Recover.
